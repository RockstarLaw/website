WHY ROCKSTAR LAW?

Learn the Systems You'll Use as a Lawyer.
Not "Pretty Close." No Hypotheticals.
Real 1:1 Simulation Learning.


[BODY COPY]


Other platforms describe legal systems. RockStar Law replicates
them.

That distinction sounds small until you see it in practice. A
trademark filing module that "covers the major fields" of a USPTO
application is not the same as a faithful replica of the actual
USPTO Trademark Center — same forms, same validation rules, same
buried buttons, same dated UX. A summary of how Form D works is
not the same as filing one through a faithful EDGAR replica that
behaves the way the real EDGAR behaves. A walkthrough of Sunbiz
is not the same as actually completing an LLC formation through
StarBiz, our 1:1 replica of the Florida Division of Corporations.

Your students will use the real systems on day one of practice.
They should train on the real systems before they get there.


WHAT 1:1 ACTUALLY MEANS

Every RockStar Law module is built from the actual government
system it replicates. We capture the real interface, the real
field structure, the real validation rules, the real page flow,
the real terminology — and we recreate them faithfully in a
training environment where students can fail, retry, and develop
fluency without a client's clock running.

That includes the parts other platforms quietly fix.

Government filing systems are not famous for their design. They
are dated. They are awkward. Their navigation is sometimes
inscrutable. Their terminology shifts between systems and
sometimes within them. Their forms can feel like they were last
updated when floppy disks were a current technology.

We replicate the awkwardness. Deliberately.

A graduate who has trained on a clean, modernized version of
Sunbiz will be slowed down on every line of the actual Sunbiz on
day one of practice. A graduate who has trained on a faithful
replica recognizes the patterns immediately. The familiarity
transfers because the interface is the same.

This is the platform's defining principle and the reason it
works: the awkwardness of government UX is not a bug to be fixed.
It is the actual training surface.

A GROWING LIBRARY OF PRACTICE PROJECTS

Drafted by working entertainment, IP, and business attorneys
based on real matters. Contract drafting. Multi-party
negotiation with confidential role-specific materials. Client
counseling memoranda with rich, ambiguous fact patterns.
Issue-spotting across IP, contract, agency, and business law.

Each project arrives with everything a professor needs:
fact pattern, confidential role materials where applicable,
deliverable specifications, supporting documents, and grading
rubrics built by attorneys who have done the work.

THREE PROFESSOR-CONTROLLED MODES

The interface never changes. The support layer does.

  GUIDED   — Full hints visible. Definitions, warnings, examples.
  ASSISTED — Hints on demand. Errors trigger contextual feedback.
  EXAM     — No hints. Real-world conditions.

Same module, three pedagogical postures. Same tool can serve
introduction, mid-course practice, and final assessment.

AI-ASSISTED GRADING ON RUBRICS BUILT BY PRACTITIONERS

Field-level accuracy on filings. Substantive evaluation on
memoranda and contracts. Rubrics refined through real student
submissions. Final grading authority always rests with the
professor.

INTELLIGENT ROSTER MATCHING

Students register under the names they actually go by. The
system matches them to professor rosters using fuzzy logic that
handles the inevitable variations between official enrollment
records and real-world preferences. Professors see both names
where it matters; students see their preferred name throughout.

INSTITUTIONAL-GRADE INFRASTRUCTURE

FERPA-compliant data handling under our institutional licensing
agreement. Roster integration with existing systems. Free
professor accounts. Student subscriptions individually or
through institutional licensing.


WHAT MAKES THIS DIFFERENT

There are other simulation platforms in legal education. Most
take one of two approaches:

  - They build generic legal "exercises" disconnected from any
    specific real-world system, leaving students to translate the
    abstraction into actual practice on their own.

  - They build clean, modernized interfaces that feel impressive
    in a demo but do not match the actual systems students will
    encounter — meaning the training does not transfer.

RockStar Law takes neither approach.

We replicate the actual systems. We preserve the actual UX,
including the parts that are not pretty. We pair the operational
layer with practice projects drawn from real matters that teach
strategic judgment, client counseling, and the kind of
"reading-into-tone" skills no casebook captures.

The result is training that transfers — directly, on day one,
without a translation step.

If you want both, you want RockStar Law.

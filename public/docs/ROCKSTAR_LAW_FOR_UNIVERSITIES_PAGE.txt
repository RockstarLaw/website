compete for jobs.

RockStar Law gives your faculty a turnkey simulation

INFRASTRUCTURE AND COMPLIANCE

RockStar Law is built for institutional adoption. Our standard
institutional licensing agreement designates RockStar Law as a
school official under FERPA with legitimate educational interest,
with the access controls, retention policies, and data handling
practices that posture requires. Our accessibility team
coordinates with your disability services office on
accommodations. Roster integration works with your existing
student information systems.

Detailed compliance documentation, security architecture,
licensing terms, and faculty adoption case studies are available
in the RockStar Law Institutional White Paper.

PLUS A SAMPLE CLASSROOM-READY PROJECT — "Who Is Moon Man?"

Moon Man is one of the projects in the RockStar Law library, and
it shows what makes our practice simulations different from
anything else in legal education. On the surface, the project
teaches contract law, copyright, trademark, and business entities
— the doctrinal areas a faculty member would expect from a fact
pattern involving a DJ persona, a divorced co-founder, and a
seven-figure merchandise business.

But Moon Man teaches more than doctrine. It teaches the
experiential skills that distinguish a competent lawyer from one
clients return to for the rest of their careers: reading an
adversary, and sometimes reading the client; understanding what
a client actually wants beneath what they say they want;
recognizing when a case is stacked against you; and knowing
whether the right move is to give up, or to figure out how to
create value where none appeared to exist.

These are the skills no casebook captures and no lecture can
deliver. They are built by repetition under realistic
constraints — which is exactly what RockStar Law does.

Hand the Moon Man project to a faculty member who teaches in
entertainment, IP, or business law. Let them read it. Then ask
them whether they could teach with it.

A member of our institutional team will follow up within two
business days to answer questions and discuss next steps.

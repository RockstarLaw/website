Welcome to the RockStar Law Security Center. RockStar Law is a legal
education simulation platform used by law students, professors, and
institutions. Because the Platform handles student personal
information, academic submissions, payment data, and accommodation
records, security matters. This Security Center explains how we
protect your account and your data, how to recognize suspicious
activity, and how to report security concerns.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. PROTECT YOUR ACCOUNT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Your RockStar Law account contains personal information, your work
product, your grades, and your communications with your professor.
Protecting it is your first line of defense.

USE A STRONG, UNIQUE PASSWORD

Your RockStar Law password should:

  - Be at least 12 characters long
  - Include a mix of upper-case and lower-case letters, numbers,
    and symbols
  - Be different from passwords you use on other sites — especially
    your school email and other educational platforms
  - Not contain your name, birthday, school name, or other
    information that could be guessed

Do not reuse passwords across services. If a password is compromised
on another website, attackers will try the same password on every
other account associated with your email address.

Consider using a password manager (1Password, Bitwarden, Apple
Keychain, etc.) to generate and store strong, unique passwords.

ENABLE TWO-FACTOR AUTHENTICATION (2FA)

When two-factor authentication is available, enable it. With 2FA,
even if someone obtains your password, they cannot access your
account without a second verification step (typically a code sent
to your phone).

PROTECT YOUR PHONE NUMBER

The phone number associated with your account is used for SMS
notifications and account verification. Treat it as part of your
account security:

  - Do not share verification codes with anyone, ever
  - If someone calls or texts asking you to "read back" a code, it
    is a scam
  - Update your phone number through your account settings if you
    change it

NEVER SHARE YOUR CREDENTIALS

RockStar Law staff will never ask for your password. Do not share
your password, security questions, or 2FA codes with:

  - Other students (even if they say they're trying to help)
  - Anyone claiming to be from RockStar Law
  - Anyone claiming to be from your law school
  - Anyone claiming to need access for "technical support"

Sharing credentials also violates the User Agreement and may result
in account termination.

LOG OUT WHEN YOU'RE DONE

Especially on shared computers (library terminals, classroom
machines, friend's laptop):

  - Always log out of RockStar Law when you finish a session
  - Do not select "remember me" or "stay logged in" on shared
    devices
  - Close the browser after logging out

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
2. PROTECT YOUR PRIVACY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

LIMIT WHAT YOU SHARE

When completing simulations on the Platform, use only the
placeholder data the Platform provides. Do not enter:

  - Your real Social Security number
  - Real entity names (use ROCKSTAR DEMO ENTITY, INC.)
  - Real addresses, phone numbers, or emails of third parties
  - Real client information from any actual or hypothetical client
  - Real EIN numbers, document numbers, or filing numbers

The Platform is a simulation. Real data does not improve your
training and creates privacy risks.

DO NOT SHARE YOUR WORK PRODUCT

Your simulated filings, drafts, and submissions are stored in your
account and shared with your professor. Do not:

  - Post your submissions on social media
  - Share screenshots of the Platform with non-subscribers
  - Distribute your work in ways that violate your User Agreement

This protects you from accusations of academic dishonesty (others
copying your work) and protects RockStar Law's intellectual property.

PROTECT OTHER STUDENTS' INFORMATION

If you encounter information about another student through the
Platform — accidentally or otherwise — do not share, screenshot, or
discuss it. Other students' submissions, grades, and accommodation
information are private.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
3. PROTECT YOUR DEVICES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

RockStar Law accounts are only as secure as the devices you use to
access them. A compromised device defeats even the strongest password.

KEEP YOUR DEVICES UPDATED

  - Install operating system updates as soon as they are available
  - Keep your browser updated to the latest version
  - Update your antivirus and security software regularly
  - Apply security patches promptly

LOCK YOUR DEVICES

  - Use a strong PIN, password, or biometric (fingerprint, Face ID)
    to lock your phone, tablet, and computer
  - Set automatic screen-lock after short inactivity
  - Never leave a logged-in device unattended in public

USE TRUSTED NETWORKS

When accessing RockStar Law:

  - Prefer your home or school network over public WiFi
  - If you must use public WiFi (coffee shops, airports), consider
    using a VPN
  - Verify that your connection to RockStar Law uses HTTPS (look
    for the lock icon and "https://" in the URL bar)

4. RECOGNIZE FAKE COMMUNICATIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Scammers may impersonate RockStar Law to trick students or
professors into giving up credentials, payment information, or
sensitive data. Learn what real RockStar Law communications look
like.

LEGITIMATE ROCKSTAR LAW EMAILS

Real RockStar Law emails:

  - Come from email addresses ending in @rockstar.law
  - Address you by your registered name (enrollment name or
    preferred name)
  - Reference specific account activity, not generic claims
  - Do not ask for your password
  - Do not threaten immediate account suspension to create panic
  - Do not promise unrealistic discounts, refunds, or rewards

Be especially suspicious of:

  - Emails from addresses that look almost like rockstar.law but
    aren't (rockstarlaw.com, rockstar-law.net, rockstarlaw.io, etc.)
  - Emails with urgent demands ("verify your account in 24 hours
    or lose access")
  - Emails offering free access, scholarships, or discounts that
    weren't initiated by you
  - Emails with grammatical errors or unusual phrasing
  - Emails asking you to "click here" to verify, especially if the
    link previews show a non-rockstar.law destination

VERIFYING A SUSPICIOUS EMAIL

If you receive an email and aren't sure if it's legitimate:

  1. Do not click any links in the email
  2. Do not reply to the email
  3. Open a new browser window and type rockstar.law directly
  4. Log in normally and check whether the message appears in your
     account notifications
  5. If still unsure, contact security@rockstar.law from a fresh
     email session (not by replying to the suspicious message)

LEGITIMATE ROCKSTAR LAW WEB PAGES

Real RockStar Law pages have URLs that include:

  - rockstar.law (or a subdomain like trademark.rockstar.law,
    starbiz.rockstar.law, etc.)
  - HTTPS (the lock icon in your browser, "https://" at the start
    of the URL)

Real RockStar Law pages do NOT:

  - Use shortened URLs (bit.ly, tinyurl, etc.) for login pages
  - Ask you to log in through unusual paths
  - Display payment forms outside the standard account billing flow
  - Ask for your password to "verify" account information

LEGITIMATE TEXT MESSAGES

RockStar Law sends SMS for verification codes and account
notifications. Real text messages from RockStar Law:

  - Will identify themselves as RockStar Law
  - Will not ask you to reply with codes, passwords, or personal
    information
  - Will not include urgent demands or threats

5. PAYMENT SECURITY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PROCESSING

RockStar Law uses third-party payment processors to handle credit
card transactions. RockStar Law does not store your full credit card
number. When you pay, your card information is sent directly to the
payment processor through encrypted channels.

WHAT YOU SHOULD SEE

When making a payment:

  - The page should display the RockStar Law branding
  - The URL should be on rockstar.law or the payment processor's
    domain (Stripe, etc.)
  - The connection should be HTTPS
  - The form should request only the payment information needed
    for the transaction

WHAT TO REPORT

Report immediately if you see:

  - A request to pay outside the normal RockStar Law billing flow
  - A request to wire funds, send cryptocurrency, or pay via gift
    cards (RockStar Law never accepts these forms of payment)
  - A request to pay through a different platform "to get a discount"
  - A duplicate or unexpected charge on your statement
  - A request from your professor to send payment directly to them
    (professors do not collect payment on RockStar Law's behalf)

6. WHAT WE DO TO PROTECT YOU
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

RockStar Law applies industry-standard security practices to protect
your account and data.

ENCRYPTION

  - All data transmitted between your device and RockStar Law is
    encrypted using TLS (the protocol behind HTTPS)
  - Sensitive data is encrypted at rest in our databases
  - Passwords are hashed and salted using current industry standards
  - Payment data is handled by PCI-compliant payment processors

ACCESS CONTROLS

  - Student data is accessible only to the student, their authorized
    professor(s), and RockStar Law personnel with a legitimate need
  - Professor access is limited to students enrolled in their
    courses
  - Administrative access requires multi-factor authentication and
    is logged

INFRASTRUCTURE

  - The Platform is hosted on reputable cloud infrastructure
    providers (Vercel, Supabase) with their own enterprise-grade
    security
  - Database backups are encrypted and stored separately
  - System logs are retained and monitored for unusual activity

INCIDENT RESPONSE

If a security incident affects your data, RockStar Law will:

  - Investigate promptly
  - Take steps to contain and remediate the incident
  - Notify affected users in accordance with applicable law
  - Cooperate with law enforcement and regulators as appropriate

7. RESPONSIBLE DISCLOSURE FOR SECURITY RESEARCHERS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

If you are a security researcher who has identified a potential
security vulnerability in the RockStar Law Platform, we appreciate
your help and ask you to follow responsible disclosure practices.

ELIGIBLE DOMAINS

Vulnerability reports are accepted for:

  - rockstar.law and its subdomains
  - The RockStar Law mobile applications (when launched)

Out-of-scope:

  - Third-party services we use (Vercel, Supabase, Stripe, etc.) —
    please report those to the third party directly
  - Replicas of government filing systems (these are intentionally
    designed to mirror those systems' actual behavior, including
    quirks and dated UX)
  - Issues with publicly available content that does not affect
    user data or platform integrity
  - Social engineering attacks against RockStar Law staff
  - Physical security of RockStar Law facilities

HOW TO REPORT

Submit security reports to:
  security@rockstar.law

Please include:

  - A clear description of the vulnerability
  - Steps to reproduce the issue
  - The affected URL or system component
  - Proof of concept (screenshots, code, or video) if applicable
  - Your contact information

WHAT WE ASK

  - Do not publicly disclose the vulnerability before we have had a
    reasonable opportunity to fix it
  - Do not access, modify, or delete data belonging to other users
  - Do not perform tests that could degrade Platform availability
    for other users (no DoS, no large-scale automated scanning)
  - Do not extort, threaten, or demand payment in exchange for
    keeping the vulnerability private

WHAT WE WILL DO

  - Acknowledge receipt of your report within five (5) business
    days
  - Investigate the report and provide updates
  - Notify you when the vulnerability has been resolved
  - Credit you publicly (with your permission) once the issue is
    fixed

We do not currently offer monetary bounties, but we recognize and
appreciate good-faith security research.

8. REPORTING SECURITY CONCERNS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Different concerns go to different places. Use the right channel
for the fastest response.

SUSPICIOUS ACCOUNT ACTIVITY ON YOUR ACCOUNT

If you notice activity you didn't perform — login from an unfamiliar
location, submissions you didn't make, payment charges you didn't
authorize — act immediately:

  1. Change your RockStar Law password
  2. Enable 2FA if you have not already
  3. Contact security@rockstar.law with details

PHISHING EMAILS OR FAKE WEBSITES

Forward suspicious emails to:
  security@rockstar.law

If you encounter a website impersonating RockStar Law, report the
URL to security@rockstar.law.

ACCOUNT TAKEOVER OR LOCKOUT

If you believe your account has been compromised or you cannot
access it:

  - Contact: security@rockstar.law
  - Use your institutional email when possible to help us verify
    your identity

PAYMENT DISPUTES OR FRAUDULENT CHARGES

  - Contact: billing@rockstar.law
  - For unauthorized charges, also contact your card issuer

GENERAL SECURITY QUESTIONS

  - Contact: security@rockstar.law

ABUSE OR HARASSMENT THROUGH THE PLATFORM

  - Contact: abuse@rockstar.law

9. LAW ENFORCEMENT REQUESTS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

RockStar Law cooperates with valid law enforcement requests while
protecting user privacy and complying with applicable law.

For law enforcement inquiries, including subpoenas, court orders,
and other legal process:

  Contact: legal@rockstar.law

Requests should:

  - Be served via valid legal process
  - Identify the specific account or data sought
  - Cite the legal authority for the request
  - Be made through proper channels (not directly to general
    support staff)

In emergencies involving imminent risk of death or serious physical
injury, contact legal@rockstar.law with "EMERGENCY DISCLOSURE
REQUEST" in the subject line.

User notice: Where permitted by law, RockStar Law will notify users
when their information is requested by law enforcement. Notice may
be delayed or withheld where prohibited by law (e.g., under a
non-disclosure order) or where notice would risk life, safety, or
the integrity of an investigation.

10. STUDENT-SPECIFIC SECURITY GUIDANCE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DO NOT USE ROCKSTAR LAW FOR ACTUAL FILINGS

The Platform is a simulation. Even if a RockStar Law replica looks
identical to a real government filing system, it is not connected
to that government system. Submitting information through RockStar
Law does not create any real legal record.

If you are dealing with an actual legal matter, consult a licensed
attorney and use the actual government filing system.

PROTECT YOUR ACADEMIC INTEGRITY

Sharing your account credentials, allowing another student to
submit work for you, or submitting another student's work as your
own violates the User Agreement and your law school's academic
integrity policies. Such conduct may also be a security concern
that we will report to your institution.

REPORT IF YOU OBSERVE OTHER STUDENTS' MISCONDUCT

If you observe another student attempting to gain unauthorized
access to the Platform, sharing credentials, or otherwise violating
security practices, you may report it to security@rockstar.law.
Reports will be handled confidentially and consistent with your
institution's policies.

11. PROFESSOR-SPECIFIC SECURITY GUIDANCE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PROTECT STUDENT DATA

As a professor, you have access to student submissions, grades, and
accommodation information. This data is subject to FERPA and other
privacy laws. Specifically:

  - Do not download, screenshot, or distribute student submissions
    outside the Platform
  - Do not share student grades or feedback with other students,
    family members, or third parties
  - Do not access submissions in courses you do not teach
  - Coordinate with your institution's disability services office
    on accommodation-related information

ROSTER ACCURACY

Maintain accurate roster information. Remove students who have
withdrawn from your course. Do not retain access for students no
longer enrolled.

REPORT INSTITUTIONAL CONCERNS

If you become aware of institutional-level security issues —
unauthorized access to your institutional account, suspicious
activity from administrative users, or breaches of institutional
licensing — contact security@rockstar.law and notify your
institution's IT security office.

12. INSTITUTIONAL SECURITY GUIDANCE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

For law schools and universities with institutional RockStar Law
accounts:

ADMINISTRATOR ACCOUNTS

Institutional administrator accounts have elevated privileges.
Administrators should:

  - Use unique, strong passwords
  - Enable 2FA
  - Limit administrator privileges to those who need them
  - Notify RockStar Law promptly when an administrator leaves the
    institution or changes roles

DATA PROCESSING AGREEMENTS

Institutions handling student data through RockStar Law are subject
to FERPA and the institutional licensing agreement. Specific data
processing terms are set forth in that agreement.

INTEGRATION WITH INSTITUTIONAL SYSTEMS

If your institution integrates RockStar Law with other systems
(LMS, identity provider, SSO), follow your institution's IT
security standards. Contact security@rockstar.law to discuss
integration options.

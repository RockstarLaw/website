DEPLOYMENT NOTES (FOR WEB DEVELOPMENT)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This master document is structured for drafting and review purposes.
When deployed to the RockStar Law website, the policies should be
implemented as follows:

1. SEPARATE PAGES, NOT ONE LONG PAGE

Each numbered section in this document becomes its own dedicated
page with its own URL. Do not deploy this as a single long
scrolling document.

Suggested URL structure:
  rockstar.law/legal                    → Policies index page
  rockstar.law/legal/terms              → Terms of Service
  rockstar.law/legal/privacy            → Privacy Policy
  rockstar.law/legal/acceptable-use     → Acceptable Use Policy
  rockstar.law/legal/educational        → Educational Disclaimer
  rockstar.law/legal/refunds            → Refund and Cancellation
  rockstar.law/legal/cookies            → Cookie Policy
  rockstar.law/legal/accessibility      → Accessibility Statement
  rockstar.law/legal/intellectual-property → IP Policy
  rockstar.law/legal/student-conduct    → Student Code of Conduct
  rockstar.law/legal/professor-agreement → Professor and Institution
  rockstar.law/legal/data-retention     → Data Retention
  rockstar.law/legal/dmca               → DMCA Policy
  rockstar.law/legal/coppa              → Children's Privacy
  rockstar.law/legal/ferpa              → FERPA Compliance
  rockstar.law/legal/consumer-health    → Consumer Health Data Notice
  rockstar.law/legal/contact            → Contact and Disputes

2. FOOTER LINK PRIORITIES

Not every policy belongs in the footer. The website footer should
include direct links to (at minimum):

  - Terms of Service
  - Privacy Policy
  - Consumer Health Data Notice (REQUIRED to be separately and
    conspicuously linked, per Washington MHMDA and similar laws —
    do not bury this inside the Privacy Policy link)
  - Educational Disclaimer
  - Accessibility Statement
  - Cookie Policy (if applicable based on cookie banner approach)
  - "Legal" or "Policies" link to the index page (which then links
    to all other policies)

Critical: The Consumer Health Data Notice must be a separate,
conspicuous footer link — equal in visibility to Terms of Service
and Privacy Policy. This is a statutory requirement, not a styling
choice. eBay and other large platforms follow this same pattern.

3. CROSS-REFERENCES

Where one policy references another (e.g., Privacy Policy referring
to the Cookie Policy), implement those references as hyperlinks to
the corresponding standalone page.

4. METADATA REQUIREMENTS

Each policy page must display:
  - The policy title
  - The effective date
  - The version number
  - A "last updated" date
  - A link back to the main /legal index

5. VERSION CONTROL

Each policy must be independently version-controlled. When a policy
is updated, only that policy's effective date changes — not all
policies.

6. CONSENT TRACKING

For policies requiring user consent (Terms of Service, Privacy
Policy, Consumer Health Data Notice), the system must:
  - Record the version of the policy the user accepted
  - Timestamp the acceptance
  - Re-prompt for consent when material changes occur

When RockStar Law provides services to a law school under an

  - Does not redisclose student records except as authorized by the

    Education

Rights under FERPA are exercised through the student's law school,
not directly through RockStar Law.

15. CONSUMER HEALTH DATA NOTICE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Effective Date: [DATE]

This Consumer Health Data Notice supplements the Privacy Policy and
provides specific information about how RockStar Law collects, uses,
shares, and protects consumer health data, as required by the
Washington My Health My Data Act, the Nevada Consumer Health Data
Privacy Law (SB 370), the Connecticut Data Privacy Act, and similar
state laws governing health-related personal information.

15.1 WHAT IS CONSUMER HEALTH DATA

For purposes of this Notice, "Consumer Health Data" includes
personal information that is linked or reasonably linkable to a
consumer and that identifies the consumer's past, present, or
future physical or mental health status. This includes information
relating to:

  - Diagnoses or anticipated diagnoses
  - Conditions and treatments
  - Diseases
  - Disabilities and accessibility needs
  - Mental health conditions and treatments
  - Medications
  - Reproductive or sexual health information
  - Gender-affirming care information
  - Biometric data
  - Genetic data
  - Precise location information that could indicate an attempt to
    acquire or receive health services
  - Information that identifies a consumer seeking health services
  - Any inference about a consumer's health derived from non-health
    information

15.2 CONSUMER HEALTH DATA WE COLLECT

In connection with operating the Platform, RockStar Law may collect
the following categories of Consumer Health Data:

ACCOMMODATION REQUESTS:
  When a student requests academic accommodations through the
  Platform — such as extended time on simulated exam exercises,
  flexible deadlines, alternative input methods, or other
  modifications related to a disability or health condition — we
  may collect:
    - The nature of the requested accommodation
    - Information identifying the student as a person with a
      qualifying condition
    - Documentation provided in support of the accommodation request
    - Communications about the accommodation between the student,
      RockStar Law, and the student's institution

DISABILITY-RELATED INFORMATION:
  Information voluntarily provided by users in connection with
  accessibility services, including disclosure of disabilities,
  conditions, or limitations that affect Platform use.

PHOTO/IMAGE DATA:
  The headshot photo uploaded during registration, which may
  contain visual information that could be processed as biometric
  data under certain interpretations of state law. RockStar Law does
  not currently use facial recognition technology or extract
  biometric identifiers from photos.

INSTITUTION-PROVIDED INFORMATION:
  Health-related accommodation information provided to RockStar Law
  by a user's law school or institution under an institutional
  licensing agreement.

15.3 HOW WE USE CONSUMER HEALTH DATA

RockStar Law uses Consumer Health Data only for the following
purposes:

  - To provide accommodations and accessibility services requested
    by users
  - To comply with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA),
    Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, and other applicable
    accessibility laws
  - To coordinate with institutions on accommodations consistent
    with FERPA and institutional disability services policies
  - To respond to user requests and inquiries
  - To comply with legal obligations
  - To protect the safety and rights of users

RockStar Law does NOT use Consumer Health Data for:
  - Targeted advertising
  - Profiling for commercial purposes
  - Selling to third parties
  - Marketing of health-related products or services

15.4 SHARING OF CONSUMER HEALTH DATA

RockStar Law shares Consumer Health Data only as follows:

WITH YOUR PROFESSOR:
  When a student receives an accommodation, the existence of the
  accommodation (but not necessarily the underlying medical reason)
  is shared with the professor as needed to implement the
  accommodation in the course.

WITH YOUR INSTITUTION:
  Information shared with the institution's disability services
  office or designated FERPA-authorized officials in coordination
  of accommodations.

WITH SERVICE PROVIDERS:
  Limited sharing with service providers (cloud hosting, database)
  who are bound by confidentiality obligations and may use the data
  only as needed to provide their services.

WITH LEGAL AUTHORITIES:
  When required by law, court order, or to respond to lawful
  government requests.

WE DO NOT SELL CONSUMER HEALTH DATA. We do not share Consumer
Health Data with third parties for their marketing purposes.

15.5 YOUR RIGHTS REGARDING CONSUMER HEALTH DATA

You have the following rights regarding your Consumer Health Data:

RIGHT TO CONFIRM AND ACCESS:
  You may confirm whether RockStar Law collects, shares, or sells
  Consumer Health Data about you and request access to that data.

RIGHT TO WITHDRAW CONSENT:
  Where we rely on your consent to collect or process Consumer
  Health Data, you may withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawal
  of consent may affect our ability to provide certain services,
  including accommodations.

RIGHT TO DELETE:
  You may request deletion of your Consumer Health Data, subject to
  exceptions where retention is required by law or to fulfill the
  purpose for which the data was collected (e.g., maintaining
  records of accommodations granted while a subscription was
  active).

RIGHT TO NON-DISCRIMINATION:
  RockStar Law will not discriminate against you for exercising
  your rights under this Notice or applicable law.

15.6 EXERCISING YOUR RIGHTS

To exercise your rights under this Notice, contact:
  privacy@rockstar.law

Please specify "Consumer Health Data Request" in your communication.
We will respond within the timeframes required by applicable law,
typically within 45 days. We may need to verify your identity before
processing your request.

15.7 CONSENT FOR COLLECTION

By submitting an accommodation request or other Consumer Health
Data through the Platform, you consent to RockStar Law's collection,
use, and sharing of that data as described in this Notice. You may
withdraw consent as described in Section 15.5.

15.8 DATA SECURITY

RockStar Law applies enhanced security measures to Consumer Health
Data, including:

  - Restricted access to authorized personnel only
  - Encryption of Consumer Health Data in storage and in transit
  - Access logging for audit purposes
  - Confidentiality agreements with personnel who handle the data

15.9 DATA RETENTION

Consumer Health Data is retained only as long as necessary for the
purpose for which it was collected, plus any retention required by
law. Specifically:

  - Active accommodation records: retained while the user has an
    active subscription
  - Accommodation history records: retained for 3 years after the
    subscription ends, to support institutional academic records
    requirements
  - Records subject to legal holds: retained as required by law

15.10 INTERACTION WITH FERPA

Where Consumer Health Data is part of an educational record under

  Washington residents have specific rights under the My Health My

  residents may also file complaints with the Washington Attorney
  General.

NEVADA RESIDENTS:
  Nevada residents have rights under SB 370, including the right to
  request that RockStar Law not sell or share Consumer Health Data.
  RockStar Law does not sell Consumer Health Data.

CONNECTICUT RESIDENTS:
  Connecticut residents have rights under the Connecticut Data
  Privacy Act, including additional protections for sensitive data
  categories.

OTHER STATES:
  As state consumer health data laws expand, additional rights may
  apply. Contact privacy@rockstar.law for jurisdiction-specific
  inquiries.
